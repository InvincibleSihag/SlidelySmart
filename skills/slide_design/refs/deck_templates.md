# Deck Templates

Standard structures for common presentation types. Use these as starting frameworks — adapt slide count and content to fit the specific request.

## Pitch Deck (10-12 slides)

**Audience**: Investors, partners, executives
**Tone**: Confident, forward-looking, data-backed
**Recommended theme**: `modern` or `default`

| # | Layout | Purpose | Design Notes |
|---|--------|---------|--------------|
| 1 | `title` | Company name + tagline | Bold, memorable tagline. No date needed. |
| 2 | `content` | Problem statement | Quantify the problem with a key metric. Use bold on the number. |
| 3 | `content` | Solution overview | Max 3 punchy bullets. What you do, not how it works. |
| 4 | `image_text` | Product / How it works | Image placeholder on left, explanation on right. |
| 5 | `section_header` | Traction & Results | Transition to proof. Keep minimal. |
| 6 | `blank` | Key metric (hero number) | Giant accent-colored number + label. See Recipe 1. |
| 7 | `two_column` | Market opportunity | TAM/SAM or competitive comparison. |
| 8 | `content` | Business model | How you make money — keep it simple, 3 bullets max. |
| 9 | `content` | Team / Why us | Credibility-focused bullets. Relevant experience only. |
| 10 | `content` | Roadmap / Next steps | 3 milestones with timeline. Use numbered list. |
| 11 | `blank` | The Ask | What you're raising, what it funds. Hero number style. |
| 12 | `title` | Closing CTA | "Let's Build This Together" + contact info in subtitle. |

**Layout rhythm**: title, content, content, image_text, section_header, blank, two_column, content, content, content, blank, title

## Status Update / Quarterly Report (8-10 slides)

**Audience**: Internal team, stakeholders, leadership
**Tone**: Clear, factual, progress-oriented
**Recommended theme**: `default`

| # | Layout | Purpose | Design Notes |
|---|--------|---------|--------------|
| 1 | `title` | Report title + period | Include date range in subtitle (e.g., "Q3 2025 — July to September"). |
| 2 | `content` | Executive summary | 3 key headlines as bullets. Bold the numbers. |
| 3 | `section_header` | Key Metrics | Transition to data. |
| 4 | `blank` | Hero metric | Biggest win this period. Large number, accent color. |
| 5 | `two_column` | Metrics comparison | This period vs. last, or plan vs. actual. |
| 6 | `section_header` | Highlights & Challenges | Transition. |
| 7 | `two_column` | Wins vs. Challenges | Left = highlights, right = challenges. Balanced view. |
| 8 | `content` | Next period priorities | Numbered list, 3-4 items. Action-oriented. |
| 9 | `title` | Closing | Key message or discussion prompt. Not "Thank You". |

**Layout rhythm**: title, content, section_header, blank, two_column, section_header, two_column, content, title

## Educational / Training (10-15 slides)

**Audience**: Learners, team members, general audience
**Tone**: Clear, structured, engaging
**Recommended theme**: `modern` or `dark`

| # | Layout | Purpose | Design Notes |
|---|--------|---------|--------------|
| 1 | `title` | Topic title | Engaging title. Subtitle = audience + date. |
| 2 | `content` | Learning objectives | Numbered list: "By the end, you will..." (3 items). |
| 3 | `section_header` | First concept | Transition to first major topic. |
| 4 | `content` | Concept explanation | Bullets or text. One idea per slide. |
| 5 | `image_text` | Visual example | Diagram/screenshot + explanation text. |
| 6 | `two_column` | Comparison or contrast | Before/after, right/wrong, old/new. |
| 7 | `section_header` | Second concept | Transition. |
| 8 | `content` | Concept explanation | Vary from slide 4 — use text or numbered list. |
| 9 | `content` | Code or technical detail | Code block with language metadata, or table. |
| 10 | `blank` | Key insight | Card callout (Recipe 4) with the core takeaway. |
| 11 | `section_header` | Practical application | Transition to hands-on. |
| 12 | `content` | Steps / How-to | Numbered list of action steps. |
| 13 | `content` | Key takeaways | 3-point summary (Recipe 8). |
| 14 | `content` | Resources / Further reading | Bullets with links. |
| 15 | `title` | Closing | Question or call to action. |

**Layout rhythm**: title, content, section_header, content, image_text, two_column, section_header, content, content, blank, section_header, content, content, content, title

## Project Proposal (8-10 slides)

**Audience**: Decision makers, sponsors, leadership
**Tone**: Persuasive, structured, evidence-based
**Recommended theme**: `default` or `modern`

| # | Layout | Purpose | Design Notes |
|---|--------|---------|--------------|
| 1 | `title` | Project name + subtitle | Clear, descriptive. Subtitle = team/department + date. |
| 2 | `content` | Problem / Opportunity | Quantify the gap. Use a bold metric. |
| 3 | `content` | Proposed solution | What you want to do — 3 concise bullets. |
| 4 | `two_column` | Approach | Left: what we'll build. Right: how we'll do it. |
| 5 | `section_header` | Impact & Investment | Transition to the business case. |
| 6 | `blank` | ROI / Key metric | Hero number: projected savings, revenue, or efficiency gain. |
| 7 | `content` | Timeline & milestones | Numbered list with dates. Keep to 3 milestones. |
| 8 | `content` | Resource requirements | What you need: budget, headcount, tools. Bullets. |
| 9 | `two_column` | Risks & mitigations | Left: risks. Right: mitigations. Shows you've thought it through. |
| 10 | `title` | Decision / CTA | "Ready to Approve?" + next steps in subtitle. |

**Layout rhythm**: title, content, content, two_column, section_header, blank, content, content, two_column, title

## Choosing the Right Template

If the user's request doesn't match a template exactly:

1. **Identify the closest template** — Most requests map to one of these four types.
2. **Adapt the structure** — Add, remove, or reorder slides to fit the specific content.
3. **Maintain layout rhythm** — Vary layouts every 2-3 slides. Don't let `content` dominate.
4. **Always include**: Strong opening (title), section transitions (section_header), and a clear closing (title with CTA or summary).
5. **Scale to content**: A simple topic might need 6-8 slides. A deep dive might need 12-15. Don't pad or compress — let the content dictate the count.
